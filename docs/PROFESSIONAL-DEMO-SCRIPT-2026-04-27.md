## Google Doc Copy-Paste Version

InfraFlow ERP Demo Script  
Suggested duration: 20 to 30 minutes

1. Opening
Thank you for taking the time today. I’ll walk you through InfraFlow ERP, which is designed to help construction and operations-focused companies manage plants, vehicles, dispatch, reporting, and operational control from one system.

2. Problem Framing
Most companies in this space face the same issues:
- data is scattered across WhatsApp, registers, and spreadsheets
- dispatch tracking is difficult
- management reports are delayed
- operational visibility depends on manual follow-up
- owner control becomes weaker as teams grow

InfraFlow ERP is designed to reduce that chaos and give structured daily visibility.

3. Login and Role View
Show:
- owner portal login
- client/company login

Explain:
- owner-level access is separated from company operations
- each company’s data remains scoped and isolated

4. Dashboard
Show:
- summary dashboard
- operational overview

Explain:
- this gives management a quick daily snapshot
- helps detect pending activity and current workload faster

5. Masters and Setup
Show:
- plants
- vehicles
- parties
- materials
- employees

Explain:
- these are the control layers that keep daily workflows clean and standardized

6. Daily Workflow Demo
Show one full example:
- create a dispatch
- update dispatch status
- verify records in the list
- open report pages

Explain:
- the software is designed for daily repeatable business actions, not just static record keeping

7. Reports
Show:
- dispatch reports
- project reports
- plant / crusher reports

Explain:
- owners and managers can review activity without waiting for end-of-day manual compilation

8. Control and Reliability
Explain:
- role-based access exists
- company scope is isolated
- production deployment is complete
- staging and load validation were completed before rollout

9. Commercial Close
Suggested line:
If this fits your operations, the next step is controlled onboarding of your company, setup of your masters, owner credential handover, and a monitored go-live.

10. Closing Questions
Ask:
- Which modules matter most to you first?
- How many plants, vehicles, or teams do you want to onboard initially?
- Do you want phased rollout or full rollout?
